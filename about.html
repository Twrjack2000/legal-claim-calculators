About This Site

Last updated: April 2025

Our Mission

Legal Claim Calculators was built with one goal: to help everyday people understand their legal rights and the value of their claims before they walk into an attorney's office — or decide that one isn't necessary.

We believe that access to legal information should not be gatekept by expensive consultations or buried in legal jargon. Our tools use the same formulas and frameworks that attorneys and insurance adjusters use, presented in a way anyone can understand.

Our Tools

We currently offer 7 free legal calculators:

  • Personal Injury Settlement Estimator — Uses the multiplier method to estimate accident claim values
  • Workers' Compensation Calculator — Based on state AWW benefit formulas and impairment schedules
  • SSDI Benefit Estimator — Uses the SSA's official AIME/PIA formula with 2025 bend points
  • DUI/DWI True Cost Calculator — Itemizes all real costs of a DUI conviction by state category
  • Wrongful Termination Estimator — Based on Title VII/ADEA damage frameworks and salary multipliers
  • Alimony Calculator — Income-gap based estimate with state-approach and marriage-length adjustments
  • Non-Compete Enforceability Checker — 10-question quiz scoring against court enforceability standards

How We Build Our Calculators

Each calculator is based on publicly available legal frameworks: statutory benefit schedules, federal sentencing guidelines, SSA actuarial data, Title VII damage caps, and published legal research on settlement averages. We do not invent numbers — we codify the same analytical frameworks used by legal professionals.

All calculations happen entirely in your browser using JavaScript. We do not receive, transmit, store, or have access to any data you enter into our calculators.

Important Limitations

Our calculators produce estimates, not legal advice, and are intended for educational purposes only. Settlement values and outcomes in real cases depend on dozens of factors our tools cannot capture: specific contract language, witness credibility, the quality of your attorney, the specific judge or jury, the employer's financial situation, and much more.

Do not make legal decisions based solely on the output of any calculator on this site. Always consult a licensed attorney in your jurisdiction before taking legal action.
