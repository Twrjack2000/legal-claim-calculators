How Personal Injury Settlements Are Calculated

Personal injury settlements compensate victims for two categories of harm: economic damages (medical bills, lost wages, property damage) and non-economic damages (pain and suffering, emotional distress, loss of enjoyment of life).

The most widely used method for calculating pain and suffering is the multiplier method: your total economic damages are multiplied by a factor between 1.5├ГтАФ and 5├ГтАФ based on injury severity. Minor soft-tissue injuries typically use 1.5├втВмтАЬ2├ГтАФ; serious injuries requiring surgery use 2.5├втВмтАЬ3.5├ГтАФ; catastrophic or life-altering injuries can use 4├втВмтАЬ5├ГтАФ or higher.

Comparative negligence reduces your award by your percentage of fault. Most states use modified comparative negligence ├втВмтАЭ if you're more than 50% at fault, you cannot recover. A few states (Alabama, Maryland, North Carolina, Virginia, and D.C.) use contributory negligence ├втВмтАЭ any fault on your part bars recovery entirely.

Insurance policy limits are a major practical constraint. No matter how large your damages, recovery is capped at the at-fault party's policy limits unless they have personal assets worth pursuing. This is why having adequate underinsured motorist (UIM) coverage on your own policy matters.
